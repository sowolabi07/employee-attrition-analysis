# employee-attrition-analysis

📊 Employee Attrition Analysis (Power BI)
🔎 Overview

This project analyzes employee attrition to uncover key drivers of turnover and identify high-risk employee segments.
The dashboard was built using Power BI, leveraging DAX, data modeling, and interactive visuals to deliver actionable business insights.

🎯 Objectives
Understand why employees leave
Identify key drivers of attrition
Segment employees to find high-risk groups
Present insights in a clear, interactive dashboard
🛠 Tools & Technologies
Power BI Desktop
DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
Data Modeling & Relationships
Data Cleaning & Transformation
Data Visualization & Storytelling
📂 Dashboard Structure
📌 Page 1: Executive Overview
Attrition Rate, Total Employees, Total Resigned
Attrition distribution (Resigned vs Stayed)
Overtime impact on attrition
📌 Page 2: Drivers of Attrition

Analysis of key factors influencing attrition:

💰 Income → Low-income employees have significantly higher attrition (~2.5×)
⏳ Tenure → Employees in early years show the highest attrition (~30%)
😊 Job Satisfaction → Lower satisfaction correlates with higher attrition
🚗 Distance from Home → Longer commute increases attrition
📈 Years Since Last Promotion → Non-linear relationship with attrition
📌 Page 3: High-Risk Employee Segmentation
Combined analysis using Age Group + Job Satisfaction
Matrix and clustered chart for deeper insights

🚨 Key Finding:

Employees under 30 with low job satisfaction (~35%) have the highest attrition risk

📊 Key Insights
Attrition is highest among:
Low-income employees
Early-career employees
Employees with low job satisfaction
Commute distance impacts retention
Promotion timing shows a non-linear pattern
Young employees + low satisfaction = highest-risk group
💼 Business Recommendations
Improve early-career onboarding and engagement
Review compensation structure for lower-income roles
Enhance employee satisfaction and workplace experience
Offer flexible work options for long-distance commuters
Optimize career growth and promotion pathways
